## Instructions

<ol>
<li itemprop="recipeInstructions" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/HowToStep"><span itemprop="text">Divide the beef into 8 loose balls, about 4 oz each.</span></li>
<li itemprop="recipeInstructions" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/HowToStep"><span itemprop="text">Heat the Blackstone to high.</span></li>
<li itemprop="recipeInstructions" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/HowToStep"><span itemprop="text">Place the beef balls on the hot griddle and smash thin.</span></li>
<li itemprop="recipeInstructions" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/HowToStep"><span itemprop="text">Season with salt and pepper.</span></li>
<li itemprop="recipeInstructions" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/HowToStep"><span itemprop="text">Flip after 2 to 3 minutes, when a crust has formed.</span></li>
<li itemprop="recipeInstructions" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/HowToStep"><span itemprop="text">Add American cheese and cook until melted.</span></li>
<li itemprop="recipeInstructions" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/HowToStep"><span itemprop="text">Toast the buns and assemble burgers with onion and pickles.</span></li>
<li itemprop="recipeInstructions" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/HowToStep"><span itemprop="text">Reserve burger patties for Burger Bowls.</span></li>
</ol>

## Planned Transformation

This dinner is designed to become a Burger Bowl for a mixed warm-and-cold work-box meal.

### Additional Ingredients

- Romaine
- Pickles
- Onion
- Optional tomato
- Burger sauce

### Burger Sauce

- Mayonnaise
- Ketchup
- Dijon
- Splash of pickle juice
- Black pepper

### Before Leaving for Work

1. Chop the burger into bite-size pieces.
2. Pack the burger separately from the vegetables.
3. Pack burger sauce separately.

### At Work / At 2:00 AM

1. Warm the burger briefly.
2. Build the bowl with romaine, pickles, onion, and tomato if using.
3. Add the warm burger.
4. Drizzle with burger sauce.

## Storage and Leftovers

- Refrigerate burger patties separately from lettuce and sauce.
- Burger bowls are best when the meat is warmed and the vegetables stay cold.
