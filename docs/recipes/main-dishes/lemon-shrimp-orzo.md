# <span itemprop="name">Lemon Shrimp Orzo</span>

<span itemprop="description">A quick lemon shrimp orzo with zucchini and Parmesan. This dinner is designed to become Chilled Lemon Shrimp Orzo Salad for a work-box meal. Version: 0.3.0.</span>

<meta itemprop="recipeCategory" content="Main dish">
<meta itemprop="recipeCuisine" content="Mediterranean-inspired American">
<meta itemprop="prepTime" content="PT15M">
<meta itemprop="cookTime" content="PT20M">
<meta itemprop="totalTime" content="PT35M">
<meta itemprop="recipeYield" content="4 servings, plus leftovers for 2 to 4 lunches">
<meta itemprop="keywords" content="shrimp, orzo, lemon, summer, planned transformation, work-box friendly">

## Servings and Time

- Servings: <span itemprop="recipeYield">4, plus leftovers for 2 to 4 lunches</span>
- Prep time: 15 minutes
- Cook time: 20 minutes
- Total time: about 35 minutes

## Ingredients

- <span itemprop="recipeIngredient">1 lb frozen shrimp, thawed</span>
- <span itemprop="recipeIngredient">12 oz orzo</span>
- <span itemprop="recipeIngredient">2 zucchini, diced</span>
- <span itemprop="recipeIngredient">2 tbsp butter</span>
- <span itemprop="recipeIngredient">1 tbsp olive oil</span>
- <span itemprop="recipeIngredient">2 tbsp jarred minced garlic</span>
- <span itemprop="recipeIngredient">Juice and zest of 1 lemon</span>
- <span itemprop="recipeIngredient">3/4 cup grated Parmesan</span>
- <span itemprop="recipeIngredient">Salt and pepper, to taste</span>

### Side

- Roasted broccoli

## Instructions

### Cook the Orzo

<ol>
<li itemprop="recipeInstructions" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/HowToStep"><span itemprop="text">Cook the orzo in salted water until al dente.</span></li>
<li itemprop="recipeInstructions" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/HowToStep"><span itemprop="text">Drain and reserve a little pasta water if needed.</span></li>
</ol>

### Cook the Zucchini and Shrimp

<ol>
<li itemprop="recipeInstructions" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/HowToStep"><span itemprop="text">Heat olive oil in a large skillet.</span></li>
<li itemprop="recipeInstructions" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/HowToStep"><span itemprop="text">Sauté the zucchini until lightly browned, then remove from the skillet.</span></li>
<li itemprop="recipeInstructions" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/HowToStep"><span itemprop="text">Cook the shrimp for 1 to 2 minutes per side, then remove from the skillet.</span></li>
</ol>

### Finish the Orzo

<ol>
<li itemprop="recipeInstructions" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/HowToStep"><span itemprop="text">Melt the butter in the skillet.</span></li>
<li itemprop="recipeInstructions" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/HowToStep"><span itemprop="text">Cook the minced garlic briefly until fragrant.</span></li>
<li itemprop="recipeInstructions" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/HowToStep"><span itemprop="text">Stir in the lemon juice, lemon zest, orzo, zucchini, shrimp, and Parmesan.</span></li>
<li itemprop="recipeInstructions" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/HowToStep"><span itemprop="text">Season to taste with salt and pepper.</span></li>
<li itemprop="recipeInstructions" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/HowToStep"><span itemprop="text">Serve with roasted broccoli and reserve leftovers for Chilled Lemon Shrimp Orzo Salad.</span></li>
</ol>

## Planned Transformation

This dinner is designed to become Chilled Lemon Shrimp Orzo Salad for a cold work-box meal.

### Additional Ingredients

- Lemon wedge
- Parmesan
- Optional chopped parsley
- Optional feta
- Optional diced cucumber
- Optional drizzle of olive oil

### Before Leaving for Work

1. Portion the shrimp orzo into a shallow meal-prep container.
2. Refrigerate until leaving for work.
3. Pack the lemon wedge and Parmesan separately.

### At Work / At 2:00 AM

1. Eat cold.
2. Squeeze fresh lemon over the top.
3. Add Parmesan.
4. Toss gently.
5. Add a drizzle of olive oil if needed.

## Storage and Leftovers

- Chill shrimp orzo promptly.
- Best eaten within 1 to 2 days.
- Add fresh lemon after chilling to brighten the flavor.
